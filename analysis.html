Attitudes toward immigration respond to brief encounters with speakers of another language, at least in the short run. Using data from Ryan Enos’s January 2012 randomized experiment on Boston-area Metra platforms (115 commuters, randomly assigned to exposure or non-exposure to Spanish-speaking confederates for two weeks), we estimate the average causal effect of that exposure on anti-immigration attitudes. However, since the experiment was conducted exclusively on Boston commuter platforms in 2012, the estimated effect may not generalize to other cities or the current political climate, where attitudes toward immigration have shifted considerably.
